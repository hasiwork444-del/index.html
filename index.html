💉 Stopping Ozempic — Key Strategy
⌄

When stopping semaglutide, hunger will return within 1–2 weeks. Here's how to fight it:

  • Psyllium husk before meals — mimics Ozempic's gastric slowing effect
  • High protein — your most powerful satiety tool (aim 135–145g/day)
  • Drink 400ml water 20–30 min before every meal
  • Always eat solid food at lunch — liquid meals digest too fast
  • Never eat from a bag or container — always portion out
  • Eat slowly: put your fork down between bites
  • The first 3–4 weeks are the hardest. Lock in habits and you'll be fine.
🔢 Your Calorie & Macro Targets
⌄

Daily calorie target: 1,500–1,600 calories (500–600 cal deficit)

Protein: 135–145g per day (critical for muscle preservation)

Carbs: 140–160g per day

Fat: 45–55g per day

Your BMR is ~1,440 cal. With your activity level (gym + swimming), maintenance is ~1,950–2,100 cal. A 500–600 cal daily deficit is the sweet spot — aggressive enough to lose weight, sustainable enough to preserve muscle.

💊 Supplement Guide
⌄
  • Creatine 5g/day — most researched supplement. Preserves muscle during deficit. Doesn't cause bloating at this dose.
  • Vitamin D3+K2 — take together with food. K2 directs calcium to bones not arteries. Almost certainly deficient.
  • Magnesium Glycinate — improves sleep, lowers cortisol, reduces cravings. Glycinate form is gentlest.
  • Omega-3 Fish Oil — reduces inflammation, supports insulin sensitivity. Take with meals to avoid aftertaste.
  • Psyllium Husk — your hunger control tool. 5g in water before meals.
  • Probiotic — Ozempic affects gut motility. Help restore balance.
😴 Lifestyle Factors
⌄

Sleep (non-negotiable): 7–9 hours. Poor sleep increases hunger hormones by 15–20% and will undo your calorie deficit.

Stress: High cortisol = fat storage, especially belly fat. Even 10 min of deep breathing after stressful events measurably reduces cortisol. Hot yoga once a week would be excellent.

Weigh yourself: Once per week, Monday morning, after bathroom, before eating. NOT daily — scales fluctuate 1–2kg daily from water alone.

Meal timing: Try to eat within a 10–12 hour window. If breakfast at 8am, finish dinner by 7–8pm.

When progress slows: It will, around weeks 6–8. This is normal physiology, not failure. Stay consistent.

💡 Tips for Success
⌄
  • Meal prep Sunday: Cook proteins and chop veg for the week. Removes daily decision-making.
  • Creatine loading: Don't bother with a loading phase — just take 5g daily from Day 1.
  • Progressive overload: Every 2 weeks, increase weights by 2.5–5kg on major lifts.
  • Rep tempo: 2 sec down, 1 sec hold, 1 sec up. Slower = more muscle activation.
  • Rest between sets: 45–60 seconds (keeps heart rate up for more calories burned).
  • Hot yoga: If you can add it back even once a week — excellent for cortisol and flexibility.
  • Progress photos: Take them every 2 weeks. The scale lies; photos don't.
  • Protein first: At every meal, eat your protein source first, then veg, then carbs.
